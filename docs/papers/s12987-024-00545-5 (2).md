## One-sentence summary

CPPs and BBB peptide shuttles (BBBpS) are **not equivalent**: by meta-analyzing 71 known BBBpS and screening 521 CPPs against nine physicochemical hallmarks, the authors identify four new brain-penetrating peptides and show that only ~2.7% of CPPs match BBBpS property profiles.

---

## 1. Problem it solves

Brain delivery is a major bottleneck in neuropharmacology (~98% of drugs cannot cross the BBB). BBB peptide shuttles (BBBpS) can translocate the BBB, but their molecular determinants were poorly characterized.

The field has often assumed that **cell-penetrating peptides (CPPs) automatically behave as BBBpS** because both cross membranes. This paper tests that assumption rigorously and finds it largely false.

**Central question:** *What turns a CPP into a BBBpS?*

---

## 2. Key advances (contributions)

1. **Curated databases** — 71 BBBpS and 521 CPPs compiled from 33 journals + review papers.

2. **Nine physicochemical hallmarks** — Quantitative molecular determinants of BBB penetration defined from the BBBpS database.

3. **60% inclusion intervals** — Statistical cutoffs per property to separate BBBpS-like from non-BBBpS-like peptides.

4. **OLS ranking score** — Simple, interpretable metric to rank CPPs by similarity to known BBBpS.

5. **Experimental validation** — 10 predicted BBBpS + 3 negative controls tested in vitro (HBEC-5i BBB model) and in vivo (mouse biodistribution with ⁶⁷Ga-labeled peptides).

6. **Four new BBBpS discovered** — BBBpS_1, BBBpS_2, BBBpS_5, BBBpS_9 outperform literature standards (TAT, penetratin, Angiopep-2, PepH3) in brain uptake.

7. **Paradigm shift** — CPP internalization (cytoplasm/nucleus) and BBB transcytosis are distinct phenomena requiring different peptide properties.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Five-step workflow (Fig. 1)

```
Step A: Database design
  → 71 BBBpS + 521 CPPs from literature search (33 journals)

Step B: Compute 9 physicochemical parameters per peptide

Step C: Define 60% inclusion interval for each parameter (BBBpS database)

Step D: Screen CPP database → peptides within all 9 intervals
  → 14 CPPs qualify (~2.7% of 521)

Step E: OLS ranking → select top 10 BBBpS candidates + 3 worst controls
  → synthesize, test in vitro and in vivo
```

### 3.2 Nine physicochemical parameters

Computed from sequence using bioSYNTHESIS peptide predictor (or literature):

| # | Parameter | Symbol in paper |
|---|-----------|-----------------|
| a | Molecular weight (g/mol) | MW |
| b | UV–Vis extinction coefficient (M⁻¹·cm⁻¹) | ε₂₈₀ (proxy for aromatic residues) |
| c | Hydrophobic residue percentage (%) | % hydrophobic |
| d | Isoelectric point | pI |
| e | Net charge at pH 7.0 | Q₇ |
| f | Total charge | \|Q\| |
| g | Average hydrophobicity | H̄ |
| h | Hydrophobicity at pH 7.0 | H₇ |
| i | Hydrophilic residue ratio (%) | % hydrophilic |

> These are the nine properties you flagged in `a.txt` as important to extract.

### 3.3 BBBpS molecular hallmarks (from 71-entry meta-analysis)

| Feature | Typical value | Interpretation |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **Small size** | Mean MW ≈ 2046 g/mol | Short peptides preferred |
| **Few aromatics** | Mean ε₂₈₀ ≈ 3790 M⁻¹·cm⁻¹ | ~1–2 Tyr or 0–1 Trp |
| **Slightly hydrophobic** | Mean ~35% hydrophobic residues | Not highly polar, not highly lipophilic |
| **Slightly cationic** | Mean net charge ≈ +2 at pH 7 | Mild positive charge (example 60% interval: −1.0 to +4.7) |

**60% intervals:** For each parameter, a symmetric range around the BBBpS mean enclosing 60% of known BBBpS entries. Full cutoffs in Table S4 (supplementary).

### 3.4 OLS ranking score

Peptides passing all nine cutoffs are ranked by distance to the BBBpS centroid:

$$S = \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left(\frac{P_{i,\text{CPP}} - \bar{P}_{i,\text{BBBpS}}}{\sigma_{i,\text{BBBpS}}}\right)^2$$

where $N = 9$, $P_{i,\text{CPP}}$ is parameter $i$ for a candidate CPP, and $\bar{P}_{i,\text{BBBpS}}$, $\sigma_{i,\text{BBBpS}}$ are the mean and standard deviation in the BBBpS database.

- **Lower S** → more BBBpS-like → better predicted translocation.
- **Higher S** → less BBBpS-like → negative control.

### 3.5 Experimental validation

| Assay | Model | Readout |
|-------|-------|---------|
| BBB translocation | HBEC-5i monolayer (in vitro BBB) | % basolateral translocation (CF-labeled) |
| Cytotoxicity | HeLa, MDA-MB-231, Hs68, HEK-293, HBEC-5i | IC₅₀ (CellTiter-Blue) |
| Internalization | Same cell panel | Relative fluorescence intensity (RFI) |
| Biodistribution | CD1 mice, tail vein | % injected dose per gram (% ID/g) at 2 min and 1 h |

Reference standard: **PepH3** (well-characterized BBBpS).

## 4. Key results

### 4.1 CPP ≠ BBBpS

- Only **14 of 521 CPPs** (~2.7%) passed all nine physicochemical filters.
- **98% of CPPs excluded** — strong evidence that CPP and BBBpS families barely overlap.
- High CPP internalization does **not** predict BBB translocation (non-BBBpS internalize strongly but cross poorly).

### 4.2 In vitro BBB translocation (24 h, 5 µM)

| Peptide | Type | Translocation (%) | S score (rank) |
|---------|------|-------------------|----------------|
| BBBpS_1 (KYKGAIIGNIK) | Candidate | **61.4 ± 2.3** | Best |
| BBBpS_5 (HIQLSPFSQSWR) | Candidate | **53.0 ± 4.7** | — |
| PepH3 (reference) | Known BBBpS | 54.0 ± 2.2 | — |
| BBBpS_2 (KYRSGAITIGY) | Candidate | 41.4 ± 3.9 | — |
| BBBpS_9 (NRPDSAQFWLHH) | Candidate | 46.0 ± 4.3 | — |
| BBBpS_7 | Candidate | 20.3 ± 5.1 | — |
| non-BBBpS_2 | Control | 2.6 ± 1.2 | Worst |
| non-BBBpS_3 | Control | 11.1 ± 2.6 | — |

- **9/10** candidates showed moderate-to-high translocation.
- **Strong negative correlation** between S score and experimental translocation (Fig. 2).
- No peptide disrupted tight junctions (HBEC-5i integrity > 90%).

### 4.3 In vivo brain uptake (2 min post-injection, ⁶⁷Ga-labeled)

| Peptide | Brain uptake (% ID/g) | vs non-BBBpS_2 (0.27) |
|---------|----------------------|------------------------|
| **BBBpS_5** | **0.74 ± 0.01** | 2.7× |
| **BBBpS_1** | **0.66 ± 0.16** | 2.4× |
| **BBBpS_9** | **0.61 ± 0.11** | 2.3× |
| **BBBpS_2** | **0.52 ± 0.23** | 1.9× |
| non-BBBpS_2 | 0.27 ± 0.04 | — |
| Literature BBBpS (TAT, penetratin, Angiopep-2, PepH3, etc.) | 0.25–0.50 | — |
| FC5 antibody fragment (reference) | 0.50 | — |

**All four validated BBBpS exceeded 0.5% ID/g** — above the best literature benchmarks.

**Pharmacokinetic profile:**
- Rapid brain entry (within 2 min).
- Fast brain washout (< 0.1% ID/g at 1 h) — desirable for shuttles, avoids toxicity.
- High renal excretion (> 75% ID/g at 1 h).

### 4.4 Internalization vs translocation distinction

| Behavior | BBBpS | non-BBBpS (CPP) |
|----------|-------|-----------------|
| BBB translocation | High (30–60%) | Low (2–20%) |
| Cell internalization (HEK-293) | Moderate (8–200 RFI) | Very high (> 150–200 RFI) |
| HBEC-5i retention | Lower | Higher |

→ CPPs enter cells efficiently but **fail to transcytose** across the endothelial barrier.

### 5.2 Approach comparison

| Aspect | Cavaco et al. | Your thesis |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Method** | Rule-based meta-analysis + OLS | Supervised ML (ESM-2 + tabular MLP) |
| **Training data** | 71 known BBBpS (literature) | 523 peptides (B3Pred + B3DB) |
| **Output** | OLS distance score S | Calibrated probability $p_{BBB}^{cal}$ |

| **Use case** | De novo screening from sequence | Reward signal for BoltzGen/TD3B + hard filter |
| **Key insight** | CPP ≠ BBBpS (property-based) | Sequence embeddings capture patterns beyond rules |

### 5.3 BBBpS design rules vs your cyclic GSK3β peptides

Cavaco's hallmarks for BBBpS:

```
Small (MW ~2000)  +  Few aromatics  +  ~35% hydrophobic  +  Net charge ~+2
```

Your designed peptides must **additionally** satisfy:

- Cyclic constraint (disulfide or backbone cyclization).
- Substrate-groove binding (hotspots R96/R180/K205).
- ATP-cleft avoidance.
- Phosphomimetic placement (L803-mts logic).
- Developability (G3–G5 gates).

BBB permeability is necessary but not sufficient — Cavaco only addresses the delivery dimension.

## 6. How to apply it to your thesis

### 6.1 Direct applications (high priority)

#### A) Use Cavaco's nine parameters as interpretability anchors

Your classifier uses 24 descriptors; Cavaco's nine are the literature-validated core. For the OxML poster and thesis discussion:

- Report whether top-ranked BoltzGen candidates fall within Cavaco's 60% BBBpS intervals.
- Use as **post-hoc explainability**: "Designed peptides satisfy known BBB molecular determinants (Cavaco et al., 2024)."

#### B) Add an OLS-style rule-based baseline

Implement Cavaco's OLS score as a **baseline comparator** in `bbb_classifier`:

```python
# Pseudocode: rank candidates by Cavaco S-score
S = sum(((p_cpp - mean_bbbps) / std_bbbps) ** 2 for each of 9 params)
```

Compare against your ML classifier on the 523-peptide dataset. If ML beats OLS, that strengthens your thesis contribution.

#### C) Hard filter aligned with BBBpS hallmarks (Gate G3 extension)

Add explicit Cavaco-rule checks to Gate G3:

| Rule | Cavaco threshold (approx.) | Action |
|------|---------------------------|--------|
| MW | < ~3000 g/mol (small) | Soft penalty if exceeded |
| Aromaticity / ε₂₈₀ | Low (few Trp/Tyr/Phe) | Penalize high aromaticity |
| Hydrophobic % | ~20–50% | Reject if extreme |
| Net charge pH 7 | −1 to +5 | Reject if highly anionic |

These complement $p_{BBB}^{cal} \ge 0.6$, not replace it.

#### D) Cite in poster motivation section

> *"BBB permeability is not equivalent to cell penetration: Cavaco et al. (2024) show that only 2.7% of known CPPs match BBB shuttle physicochemical profiles, motivating a dedicated BBB classifier rather than assuming CPP-like behavior."*

### 6.2 Medium-effort applications

#### E) Composite reward with Cavaco S-score

Extend your Best-of-N / TD3B reward:

$$R_{\text{total}} = w_1 \cdot p_{BBB}^{cal} + w_2 \cdot (1 - S_{\text{norm}}) + w_3 \cdot M_{\text{hot}} - w_4 \cdot U_{\text{ATP}}$$

where $S_{\text{norm}}$ is the Cavaco OLS score normalized to [0, 1]. Combines learned (ML) and literature-based (meta-analysis) BBB signals.

#### F) Feature ablation study

Train classifier variants:

| Experiment | Features | Expected insight |
|------------|----------|------------------|
| Cavaco-9 only | 9 params | Rule-based ceiling |
| ESM-2 only | Embeddings | Sequence-level patterns |
| Cavaco-9 + ESM-2 | Fusion (your best model) | Synergy |
| Full 24 | All descriptors | Diminishing returns? |

Directly tests whether your extra 15 descriptors add value beyond Cavaco's nine.

#### G) Negative design: avoid "CPP-like but not BBBpS" peptides

Cavaco shows non-BBBpS have **high internalization but low translocation**. Flag candidates with:

- High predicted cell-penetration proxies (high basic residue %, high charge density).
- Low $p_{BBB}^{cal}$.

This is a developability liability specific to brain delivery.

## 8. Conclusions for your thesis

1. **Validates your feature engineering** — The nine properties you extract (noted in `a.txt`) are exactly what Cavaco et al. identify as BBB molecular determinants. Your 24-descriptor pipeline is well-grounded in literature.

2. **Strengthens the case for a dedicated BBB classifier** — CPP properties do not predict BBB penetration. A task-specific model (your ESM-2 + tabular fusion) is scientifically justified.
